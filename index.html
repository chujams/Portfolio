This site is currently undergoing a redesign. Feel free to send me an email for my latest works, or to chat about any opportunities you may have. I look forward to speaking with you!

Hi there, my name's Jimmy. I'm a recent graduate of University of California, Santa Cruz, with a B.S. in Technology & Information Management.

I enjoy designing things and experiences, anything from advertisements to t-shirts, websites to D&D campaigns. I have a background in multimedia design, with extensive training in the core principles of design, both technical and human-centric. I believe that good design must be not only pleasing to look at (due to careful choices in color, typography, spacing, textures, and so on) but also pleasing to use - the layout and the behavior of each element carefully considered and weighed.

I started my design education early, with a four-year magnet program in multimedia design in high school. I continued to build on that experience in university, where my degree included courses in systems analysis/design, product design/development, supply chain management, and the fundamentals of computer science and business management. Additionally, my previous design experience allowed me to take electives in UX/UI design and web design, giving me the fundamentals needed to continue to grow via self-study.

My hobbies include musical instruments (ranging from as traditional as the piano to the more outlandish such as the kalimba or melodica), fitness, writing, badminton, and gaming.

I am currently seeking all employment and internship opportunities in fields such as:
UX Design, UI Design/Development, Graphic Design, Web Design/Development, Instructional Design, etc., as well as opportunities in information technology, digital marketing, or product management.
I am currently located in San Jose, California, but I'm open to relocating for the right opportunity.

    • Showboat

    "Showboat" is an online community for discussing and tracking shows and movies. The website's goal is to make watching shows and movies (whether through cable, theatres, or a streaming service such as Netflix) a more social experience by encouraging users to participate in discussion threads on the forums, track which shows their friends are watching, discover new shows via reviews and recommendations, and keep their current shows organized by tracking which episodes they've watched.

    The site is inspired by show discussion threads on popular content aggregation site "Reddit" and animated series tracking site "MyAnimeList", combined with an internet forum and a social media site.
